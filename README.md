## Executive Summary

SlayCorp delivers complex programs using a governance-first execution model designed for high-consequence environments. Work is structured, bounded, and independently verified at every stage to ensure accountability and control.

AI is integrated as a governed execution resource within this model — accelerating delivery without removing human oversight or decision authority.

The result is a repeatable system for delivering complex work with clarity, discipline, and reduced risk.

## What This Repository Demonstrates

This is not a code portfolio. It is a controlled summary of how SlayCorp
structures and governs complex program delivery, and how AI is applied
within that model responsibly.

Three things are demonstrated here:

- **Governance-first execution** — every deliverable starts with a defined
  scope, acceptance criteria, and authority boundary before work begins
- **Structured delivery discipline** — planning, execution, and verification
  are separated by design, with independent validation at every stage
- **AI as an execution amplifier** — AI supports bounded, governed tasks
  within a human-controlled management system; it does not replace judgment
  or oversight

## Operating Model

SlayCorp organizes all delivery work through a six-phase governed cycle:

**Intake → Govern → Plan → Execute → Verify → Close**

This is a control system, not just a workflow.

Each phase has defined inputs, outputs, and accountability assignments.
Nothing advances without meeting the criteria of the current phase. This
model applies consistently regardless of program size, domain, or complexity.

The result is a delivery system where:

- scope is locked before execution begins
- execution is bounded by documented agreements
- verification is independent from the team that produced the work
- every decision produces a traceable artifact

## Why It Matters

Most delivery failures are not technical. They are failures of accountability —
unclear ownership, unverified assumptions, and decisions that cannot be
reconstructed after the fact.

SlayCorp's model addresses this directly:

- **Lower coordination risk** — clear role separation eliminates ambiguity
  about who owns what
- **Stronger accountability** — every task is tied to a documented scope and
  measurable outcome
- **Faster structured delivery** — a repeatable cycle reduces ramp-up time
  and decision fatigue across programs
- **Control in sensitive environments** — governance structures are designed
  to hold under pressure, not just under ideal conditions

Delivery record: 36 consecutive governed cycles with no rework required
across completed scopes.

## AI Integration Approach

AI is integrated into this delivery model as a governed execution resource —
not as an autonomous decision-maker and not as an uncontrolled productivity
tool.

The governing principle: **AI operates within approved boundaries. All
strategic decisions, risk tolerance, and governance rules remain under
human authority.**

In practice this means:

- AI is assigned specific, bounded tasks within the delivery cycle
- Every AI-produced output is reviewed against defined acceptance criteria
- Execution and verification are structurally separated — no self-certification
- The human program leader retains final authority at every stage

This approach allows a single senior program leader to operate with the
consistency, scale, and control of a larger team without sacrificing
accountability or oversight.
