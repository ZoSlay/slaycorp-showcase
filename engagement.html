Engagement Model
Structured for Controlled Adoption
01
Four-Phase Methodology

SlayCorp engages through a structured four-phase methodology designed to deliver measurable results while maintaining organizational control. Each phase builds on the previous one, with clear deliverables and decision points before advancing.

Phase 1
Assess
Map current workflows, identify delivery gaps, and define governance requirements
Phase 2
Implement
Deploy governed execution workflows with embedded controls and accountability structures
Phase 3
Validate
Verify outcomes against acceptance criteria using independent validation and auditable evidence
Phase 4
Scale
Expand proven patterns across the organization with documented playbooks and trained teams
02
Governed Delivery Cycle

Within each engagement phase, all delivery work follows SlayCorp's six-phase governed cycle. This model applies consistently regardless of program size, domain, or complexity.

Phase Purpose
Intake Receive and scope the work request, establish mission context and constraints
Govern Define authority boundaries, acceptance criteria, and governance rules before execution begins
Plan Decompose work into bounded contracts with explicit success criteria and agent assignments
Execute Deliver against the contract within approved scope — no self-modification, no scope expansion
Verify Independent verification against acceptance criteria by a separate agent — no self-certification
Close Archive decision artifacts, update state, and capture lessons for validated memory
Scope is locked before execution begins. · Verification is independent from execution.
03
What This Means in Practice

Lower coordination risk through clear role separation that eliminates ambiguity about who owns what. Stronger accountability where every task is tied to a documented scope and measurable outcome. Faster structured delivery through a repeatable cycle that reduces ramp-up time and decision fatigue across programs. And control in sensitive environments — governance structures designed to hold under pressure, not just under ideal conditions.

Delivery record: 48 consecutive governed cycles with no rework required across completed scopes.
